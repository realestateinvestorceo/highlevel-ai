Why Agencies Are Looking for ActiveCampaign Alternatives

ActiveCampaign has been a leading email marketing and automation platform for over a decade, but agencies in 2026 are increasingly searching for alternatives due to three recurring pain points.

Per-contact pricing that scales aggressively. ActiveCampaign's pricing is tied directly to your contact count. The Starter plan begins at approximately $15/month for 1,000 contacts, but an agency managing 25,000 contacts on the Professional plan pays around $400/month, and Enterprise pricing can exceed $800/month. Every new lead increases your bill. For agencies managing multiple client accounts with growing lists, this pricing model creates unpredictable and escalating costs.

Email-first platform with gaps elsewhere. ActiveCampaign was built as an email automation tool. While it has added a basic CRM (available on higher tiers), it still does not include a funnel builder, website builder, SMS marketing, appointment scheduling, reputation management, or AI chatbots. Agencies using ActiveCampaign typically need separate subscriptions for tools like ClickFunnels or Leadpages (for funnels), Twilio or SlickText (for SMS), Calendly (for scheduling), and a separate review management tool. These add-ons create integration complexity and raise total monthly costs well beyond the ActiveCampaign subscription alone.

No white-label SaaS mode. For agencies that want to resell marketing software under their own brand, ActiveCampaign does not offer a white-label option. GoHighLevel's SaaS Pro plan at $497/month lets agencies fully rebrand the platform, set their own pricing, and bill clients directly — turning their agency into a software company with recurring revenue.
