SWITCHING

Migrating from ClickFunnels to GoHighLevel

Step 1: Export Your Contacts

ClickFunnels allows you to export your contact list as a CSV file. Download your full contact database including names, emails, phone numbers, tags, and any custom data. Import this CSV directly into GoHighLevel's contact manager. The import process typically takes a few minutes and maps fields automatically.[3]

Step 2: Rebuild Your Funnels

ClickFunnels does not offer a direct funnel export feature, so funnels need to be recreated in GoHighLevel's drag-and-drop builder. GoHighLevel offers snapshot templates that replicate common funnel structures (webinar funnels, lead magnet funnels, sales page funnels, etc.), which accelerates the rebuild process. Many users find that GoHighLevel's builder is comparable in capability, and most funnels can be recreated within a few hours each.[3]

Step 3: Set Up Automations and Workflows

Rebuild your email sequences, follow-up automations, and any funnel-trigger actions in GoHighLevel's visual workflow builder. GoHighLevel's automation engine is more powerful than ClickFunnels' basic automation, so you will likely be able to create more sophisticated workflows than what was possible in ClickFunnels. Take the opportunity to add SMS follow-ups and AI chatbot triggers that were not available before.[3]

Step 4: Redirect Domains and Test

Point your custom domains to GoHighLevel, update any external links, and thoroughly test all funnels, forms, and automations before deactivating your ClickFunnels account. Most agencies complete the full migration within one to two weeks depending on the number of funnels and complexity of automations.
