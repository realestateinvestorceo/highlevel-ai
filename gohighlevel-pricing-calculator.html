Why Hidden Costs Matter

Every GoHighLevel review mentions the base plan price. Few mention the add-ons that turn a $97 plan into a $112-130 monthly commitment. The biggest variable is Twilio, which handles all SMS and voice features. Your cost depends entirely on message volume and call duration.

The good news: even with add-ons, GoHighLevel typically costs less than the stack of separate tools it replaces. A HubSpot CRM + Mailchimp + ClickFunnels + Calendly stack easily runs $280+/mo before you add SMS or automation.

How to Minimize Twilio Costs

  • Use email first. Reserve SMS for high-intent messages like appointment reminders and confirmations. Email is nearly free on Mailgun.
  • Keep messages under 160 characters. Longer messages split into multiple segments, each billed separately.
  • Use LC Phone as an alternative. GoHighLevel's built-in phone system (LC Phone) has slightly higher per-message rates but removes the setup complexity of Twilio. Good for low-volume users.
  • Register for A2P 10DLC early. Required for US business texting. The $19 registration fee is one-time and improves deliverability.

Which Plan Is Best for Your Use Case

  • Solo business or freelancer: Starter ($97/mo). One sub-account is all you need for a single business.
  • Agency managing clients: Unlimited ($297/mo). Unlimited sub-accounts means you can onboard clients without per-seat charges.
  • SaaS entrepreneurs: SaaS Pro ($497/mo). White-label the platform, set your own pricing, and resell it as your own branded software.
