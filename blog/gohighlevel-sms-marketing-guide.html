Setting Up Your Twilio Account and Connecting to GoHighLevel

Before you can send a single text message through GoHighLevel, you need a phone service provider. While GoHighLevel offers LC Phone (we'll cover that later), most users start with Twilio because it's been the standard integration for years.

Step 1: Create Your Twilio Account

Head to Twilio's website and create a new account. You'll need to verify your identity with a phone number and email address. Twilio will give you $15 in free credits to start testing.

Step 2: Get Your API Credentials

Once logged into Twilio, locate your Account SID and Auth Token in the upper right corner of your dashboard. These are your API credentials that allow GoHighLevel to communicate with Twilio.

Step 3: Connect Twilio to GoHighLevel

  1. In GoHighLevel, navigate to Settings → Phone Numbers
  2. Click "Add Provider" and select Twilio
  3. Enter your Account SID and Auth Token
  4. Click "Connect"

Step 4: Test the Connection

Send a test message to your own phone number to verify everything is working. Check both your phone and the communication logs in GoHighLevel to confirm delivery.

Important: Do not start any marketing campaigns until you complete A2P 10DLC registration. Sending marketing messages without proper registration can result in heavy filtering or blocked messages.

A2P 10DLC Registration: The Non-Negotiable Step

A2P (Application-to-Person) 10DLC registration is required by U.S. carriers for all business SMS messaging. This isn't optional—it's mandatory for any marketing or promotional messages.

Why A2P 10DLC Exists

Carriers implemented A2P 10DLC to reduce spam and improve message deliverability. Without registration, your messages will be filtered, blocked, or heavily throttled.

Step-by-Step A2P 10DLC Registration Process

  1. Brand Registration: Register your business brand with Twilio, including business name, EIN, website, and business type
  2. Brand Verification: Provide documentation like articles of incorporation or business licenses
  3. Campaign Registration: Define your use cases (promotional, customer service, etc.)
  4. Number Assignment: Assign your 10DLC numbers to approved campaigns

Costs and Timeline

  • Brand Registration: $4 one-time fee
  • Campaign Registration: $10 one-time fee per campaign
  • Monthly Fees: $2-15 per month depending on throughput requirements
  • Timeline: 1-3 weeks for standard brands, up to 6-8 weeks for special verification
Pro tip: Start your A2P 10DLC registration immediately, even before you're ready to send messages. The approval process takes time, and you can't send marketing messages without it.

Phone Number Setup: Local vs Toll-Free

Choosing the right phone numbers affects both deliverability and costs.

Local Numbers (10DLC)

  • Cost: $1-2 per month per number
  • Best for: Two-way conversations, customer service, appointment reminders
  • Throughput: 75-4,500 messages per day (depending on brand verification)
  • Requirements: Must complete A2P 10DLC registration

Toll-Free Numbers

  • Cost: $2-5 per month per number
  • Best for: High-volume campaigns, promotional messages
  • Throughput: Up to 1,000 messages per day initially
  • Requirements: Toll-free verification process required

In my experience, most small businesses should start with local numbers for personalized communication, then add toll-free numbers as volume increases.

TCPA Compliance: The Make-or-Break Factor

TCPA (Telephone Consumer Protection Act) violations can cost $500-1,500 per message. I've seen businesses face five and six-figure lawsuits for non-compliance. Here's what you absolutely must know:

Opt-In Requirements

Express Written Consent is required for all marketing messages. This means:

  • Clear disclosure that person is agreeing to receive text messages
  • Your business name and phone number
  • Statement that consent isn't required to purchase
  • Information about message/data rates

Example compliant opt-in: "By clicking Submit, you consent to receive marketing text messages from [Business Name] at the number provided, including messages sent by autodialer. Consent is not a condition of purchase. Message and data rates may apply. Message frequency varies. Reply HELP for help or STOP to cancel."

Opt-Out Handling

GoHighLevel automatically handles STOP requests, but you need to understand the rules:

  • Must honor STOP, CANCEL, END, QUIT, UNSUBSCRIBE, and similar keywords
  • Must stop messages within a reasonable time (typically within 5 minutes)
  • Can send one final confirmation message
  • Cannot charge for processing opt-out requests

Quiet Hours

Never send marketing messages outside 8 AM - 9 PM in the recipient's time zone. GoHighLevel can handle this automatically if you set it up correctly.

Important: TCPA compliance is your responsibility, not GoHighLevel's or Twilio's. When in doubt, consult with an attorney specializing in TCPA law.

SMS Campaign Types in GoHighLevel

GoHighLevel supports three main types of SMS campaigns, each serving different purposes:

Broadcast Messages

One-time messages sent to a list of contacts. Perfect for announcements, promotions, or event reminders. Use the Campaigns section to set up broadcasts with proper filtering and scheduling.

Drip Sequences

Automated series of messages sent over time. These work great for onboarding new customers or nurturing leads. Set these up in the automation workflows section.

Triggered Messages

Messages sent based on specific actions or events, like form submissions, appointment bookings, or website visits. These typically have the highest engagement rates because they're contextually relevant.

SMS Templates That Actually Convert

After testing thousands of messages, here are the templates that consistently perform best:

Appointment Reminders

"Hi [First Name], this is [Business Name] confirming your appointment tomorrow at [Time]. Please reply YES to confirm or RESCHEDULE if you need to change. See you soon!"

Follow-Up After Service

"Hi [First Name], thanks for choosing [Business Name] today! How was your experience? If you loved it, we'd appreciate a quick review: [Link]. Reply STOP to opt out."

Promotional Messages

"[First Name], exclusive offer for you! 20% off your next [Service] when you book by Friday. Ready to schedule? Reply YES or call [Phone]. Expires [Date]. Reply STOP to opt out."

Pro tip: Keep messages under 160 characters when possible to avoid multi-segment charges. Every 160 characters counts as one message segment.

Two-Way SMS Conversations

One of GoHighLevel's strongest features is handling two-way SMS conversations. When prospects reply to your messages, they appear in the conversations tab where your team can respond in real-time.

Best practices for two-way conversations:

  • Respond quickly (within 5 minutes if possible)
  • Use saved replies for common questions
  • Set up auto-responses for after-hours inquiries
  • Train your team on professional texting etiquette

SMS Costs Breakdown

Understanding SMS costs helps you budget effectively and avoid surprises.

Twilio Pricing Structure

  • Outbound SMS: $0.0079 per message segment
  • Inbound SMS: $0.0079 per message segment
  • Phone numbers: $1-5 per month depending on type
  • A2P 10DLC fees: $4-25 one-time plus monthly throughput fees

What Counts as a Message Segment

  • Standard SMS: 160 characters = 1 segment
  • Unicode (emojis, special characters): 70 characters = 1 segment
  • Messages over the limit split into multiple segments
  • Each segment is charged separately

Example: A 200-character message counts as 2 segments and costs $0.0158 to send.

LC Phone: The Twilio Alternative

GoHighLevel's LC Phone system launched as an alternative to third-party providers like Twilio. Here's how it compares:

LC Phone Advantages

  • Integrated directly into GoHighLevel (no separate setup)
  • Simpler compliance handling
  • Unified billing with your GoHighLevel subscription
  • Voice calling included

LC Phone Limitations

  • Less control over advanced features
  • Potentially higher costs for high-volume users
  • Newer system with fewer integration options

For most small businesses, LC Phone is the easier choice. For agencies managing multiple clients or high-volume senders, Twilio often provides better value and flexibility.

Common SMS Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

I've seen these mistakes cost businesses thousands of dollars and damage their reputations:

Mistake 1: Sending Messages Without Proper Consent

Problem: Using purchased lists or adding people who didn't explicitly opt in.
Solution: Only message people who opted in through your own forms or gave verbal consent.

Mistake 2: Ignoring Quiet Hours

Problem: Sending promotional messages at 11 PM or 6 AM.
Solution: Configure GoHighLevel to respect quiet hours (8 AM - 9 PM recipient's timezone).

Mistake 3: Not Testing Messages

Problem: Sending campaigns with broken links, wrong personalization, or formatting issues.
Solution: Always send test messages to yourself before launching campaigns.

Mistake 4: Overcomplicating Opt-In Language

Problem: Using confusing legal language that doesn't actually provide proper consent.
Solution: Use clear, simple language that explicitly states what people are agreeing to.

Mistake 5: Not Monitoring Deliverability

Problem: Continuing to send messages when delivery rates drop, indicating filtering issues.
Solution: Monitor delivery rates and investigate any significant drops.

Important: These compliance issues are serious. If you're unsure about any aspect of SMS compliance, consult with a qualified attorney before launching campaigns.
SMS marketing can be incredibly effective when done properly. The key is prioritizing compliance first, then focusing on creating valuable, timely messages that recipients actually want to receive.

Bottom Line

SMS marketing in GoHighLevel is powerful, but it requires careful attention to compliance and best practices. Start with proper A2P 10DLC registration, ensure you have explicit consent from all recipients, and focus on providing value rather than just pushing sales messages. Whether you choose Twilio or LC Phone, the fundamentals remain the same: respect your audience, follow the law, and measure your results.
