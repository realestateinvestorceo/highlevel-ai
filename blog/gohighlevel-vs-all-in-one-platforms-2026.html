GoHighLevel vs All-in-One Platforms: An Honest Comparison (2026)

How GoHighLevel stacks up against HubSpot, ClickFunnels, Kartra, Kajabi, and others — features, pricing, and which is best for your situation.

JM

Marketing Technology Consultant

After six years of using GoHighLevel and testing every major all-in-one platform on the market, I'm constantly asked the same question: "Is GoHighLevel actually better than [insert platform]?"

The honest answer? It depends on what you're trying to build.

In 2026, the all-in-one platform landscape is more crowded than ever. While GoHighLevel has carved out a dominant position for agencies, platforms like HubSpot, ClickFunnels, Kartra, and Kajabi each excel in their own niches. Let me break down exactly where each platform wins—and where they fall short.

GoHighLevel vs HubSpot: Agency Tools vs Enterprise Power

Who They're Built For

GoHighLevel was designed from the ground up for marketing agencies managing multiple clients. Everything from sub-accounts to white-labeling screams "agency first."

HubSpot targets enterprise B2B companies that need sophisticated CRM capabilities and can afford to scale up as they grow.

Key Feature Differences

The most obvious difference is in how they handle multiple clients. GoHighLevel gives you unlimited sub-accounts at $97/month—you could manage 100 clients for the same price. HubSpot charges per contact and user, so 100 clients could easily cost $3,000+ monthly.

HubSpot wins on integrations (2,000+ vs GoHighLevel's 500+) and enterprise features like advanced reporting and their new Breeze AI tools. But GoHighLevel includes native SMS, voice calling, and AI phone agents that HubSpot treats as expensive add-ons.

Pro tip: Use our detailed HubSpot comparison to see exact feature differences and cost breakdowns for your specific use case.

Real Pricing Comparison

Here's where it gets interesting. HubSpot's "free" CRM looks attractive until you need automation, custom properties, or more than 1,000 contacts. Their Professional plan starts at $800/month for 2,000 contacts and scales quickly.

GoHighLevel's $97 Unlimited plan includes everything—unlimited contacts, users, emails, SMS, and funnels. For a 10-client agency, that's potentially $2,400/month in savings.

Where Each Platform Wins

GoHighLevel wins when:

  • You manage multiple clients (agencies, consultants)
  • You want to resell the platform as your own SaaS
  • You need cost-effective SMS and voice capabilities
  • You want one platform instead of juggling multiple tools

HubSpot wins when:

  • You're a large B2B company with complex sales processes
  • You need advanced reporting and attribution
  • You require deep integrations with enterprise software
  • You have dedicated marketing and sales teams

GoHighLevel vs ClickFunnels: Complete Platform vs Funnel Master

Target Audience Split

ClickFunnels serves entrepreneurs and businesses that live and die by their sales funnels. Think course creators, coaches, and e-commerce brands focused on conversion optimization.

GoHighLevel attracts agencies and service businesses that need funnels plus CRM, communication tools, and client management.

The Funnel Building Experience

I'll be honest—ClickFunnels still builds better funnels. Their templates are more polished, their editor is more intuitive, and their conversion optimization features are top-notch. Features like smart shopping carts, one-click upsells, and A/B testing are more refined.

GoHighLevel's funnel builder has improved dramatically since 2019, but it's still more utilitarian than elegant. Where GoHighLevel shines is in what happens after someone hits your funnel—the CRM automation, SMS follow-up, and lead nurturing.

Important: ClickFunnels lacks native CRM and SMS capabilities. You'll need integrations with tools like Mailchimp or Twilio, adding complexity and cost.

Pricing Reality Check

Both platforms start at $97/month, but the value proposition differs. ClickFunnels gives you unlimited funnels and traffic but limits team members and requires integrations for CRM functionality.

GoHighLevel includes CRM, email marketing, SMS, phone systems, and basic course hosting in that same $97. For most businesses, GoHighLevel replaces 4-6 separate tools.

When to Choose Each

Choose ClickFunnels if:

  • Funnels are your primary revenue driver
  • You're comfortable using separate tools for CRM and communication
  • You need the most polished funnel templates and conversion features
  • You're focused on e-commerce or course sales

Choose GoHighLevel if:

  • You want funnels integrated with complete business management
  • You need SMS and voice capabilities
  • You manage multiple clients or want to white-label
  • You prefer one platform over multiple integrations

GoHighLevel vs Kartra: Agency Platform vs Solo Business Tool

The Philosophy Difference

Kartra is built for solopreneurs and small teams running knowledge-based businesses. Everything is designed to be simple and beginner-friendly.

GoHighLevel assumes you're managing complexity—multiple clients, team members, and business processes that require more sophisticated automation.

Feature Comparison

Kartra starts at just $59/month and includes solid automation, video hosting, affiliate management, and helpdesk functionality. It's genuinely user-friendly and perfect for coaches or consultants just getting started.

But Kartra lacks sub-accounts, white-labeling, native SMS/calling, and the scalability features that agencies need. You also hit contact limits quickly—their $59 plan caps at 2,500 leads.

Where Each Excels

Kartra wins for:

  • Beginners who want something that "just works"
  • Solo coaches and consultants
  • Businesses heavily focused on video content and courses
  • Lower-volume operations (under 10,000 contacts)

GoHighLevel wins for:

  • Agencies managing multiple clients
  • Businesses needing SMS and voice communication
  • Teams that want to white-label and resell
  • High-volume operations with unlimited scaling needs

GoHighLevel vs Kajabi: Business Platform vs Course Creation Specialist

The Course Creator's Dilemma

Kajabi is the premium choice for course creators who want everything to look polished and professional. Their templates are gorgeous, their course delivery is smooth, and their student experience is top-tier.

GoHighLevel includes basic course functionality (video hosting, quizzes, certificates) but treats it as one feature among many, not the centerpiece.

Pricing and Limitations

Kajabi starts at $89/month but limits you to 1,000 contacts and 1 website. Their Growth plan at $159/month gives you 10,000 contacts but still caps websites and products. For unlimited scaling, you need their Pro plan at $319/month.

GoHighLevel gives you unlimited everything at $97/month, including basic course hosting. The course features aren't as polished as Kajabi's, but you also get complete CRM, SMS, funnels, and agency tools.

Pro tip: Course creators often combine GoHighLevel for marketing automation and lead generation with specialized course platforms for delivery. Check our coaches guide for specific strategies.

Decision Framework

Choose Kajabi if:

  • Course creation is your primary business model
  • You want premium design and student experience
  • You're willing to pay more for specialized course features
  • You don't need agency tools or white-labeling

Choose GoHighLevel if:

  • Courses are part of a broader service business
  • You need strong CRM and automation capabilities
  • You want to minimize platform costs
  • You're building an agency or consulting business

GoHighLevel vs Keap/Infusionsoft: Modern Agency Tool vs Legacy CRM

The Legacy Challenge

Keap (formerly Infusionsoft) was revolutionary in 2005 but feels dated in 2026. While they've modernized their interface, the core architecture still reflects small business CRM thinking rather than modern all-in-one platform design.

GoHighLevel was built in the cloud-first, mobile-first era with modern agencies in mind.

Feature and Pricing Gaps

Keap's pricing starts at $99/month for 1,500 contacts, similar to GoHighLevel's $97 unlimited plan. But Keap lacks native SMS, voice calling, funnel building, course hosting, and white-labeling—all standard in GoHighLevel.

The learning curve is also dramatically different. I've watched team members get productive in GoHighLevel within days, while Keap typically requires weeks of training.

When Keap Still Makes Sense

Keap wins if you're already heavily invested in their ecosystem or need their specific CRM workflow logic. Some established businesses have years of customization that makes switching painful.

For new businesses or agencies, GoHighLevel offers more features, better usability, and superior value.

The Decision Matrix: Which Platform Fits Your Business?

After testing all these platforms extensively, here's my honest recommendation framework:

Choose GoHighLevel If:

  • You're running a marketing agency or consulting business
  • You manage multiple clients and need sub-accounts
  • You want to white-label and resell as your own SaaS
  • You need SMS, voice calling, and AI phone capabilities
  • You want unlimited contacts/users without scaling costs
  • You prefer one platform over managing multiple tool integrations
  • You're building workflows for lead generation and nurturing

Choose HubSpot If:

  • You're an enterprise B2B company with complex sales processes
  • You have dedicated marketing and sales teams
  • You need advanced attribution and reporting
  • You require deep integrations with enterprise software
  • Budget isn't a primary concern (you can afford $800+/month)
  • You prioritize platform maturity and ecosystem depth

Choose ClickFunnels If:

  • Funnels are your primary revenue driver
  • You're focused on e-commerce or course sales
  • You need the most polished funnel templates and conversion tools
  • You're comfortable using separate tools for CRM and communication
  • You're building a funnel-based business model

Choose Kartra If:

  • You're a solo entrepreneur or small team
  • You want something simple and beginner-friendly
  • Your business is heavily video/course focused
  • You have under 10,000 contacts and limited complexity
  • You need the lowest entry cost with solid automation

Choose Kajabi If:

  • Course creation is your primary business model
  • You want premium design and student experience
  • You're building a knowledge business, not a service agency
  • You're willing to pay more for specialized course features
  • Brand presentation and polish are critical to your success

The Real Cost of Platform Choice

Beyond monthly fees, consider the hidden costs of your platform choice:

Integration complexity: Platforms like ClickFunnels require additional tools for complete functionality, adding integration maintenance and potential points of failure.

Learning curve: HubSpot and Keap require significant training investment. GoHighLevel and Kartra are more intuitive but still need onboarding time.

Scaling costs: Contact-based pricing (HubSpot, Kajabi, Kartra) can explode as you grow. Unlimited pricing (GoHighLevel, ClickFunnels) provides predictable costs.

Team training: If you have multiple team members, factor in training time and ongoing support needs.

In my experience running an agency, the total cost of ownership often favors platforms with flat-rate pricing and comprehensive feature sets, even if the monthly cost seems higher initially.

Bottom Line

No platform is perfect for everyone, and that's actually a good thing—it means you can choose tools designed for your specific business model.

GoHighLevel dominates the agency space because it was built specifically for that use case. The unlimited pricing, white-labeling, and sub-account structure solve real agency problems that other platforms treat as afterthoughts.

But if you're running an enterprise B2B company, HubSpot's depth and integrations matter more than agency features. If you're a course creator, Kajabi's student experience might justify the higher cost. If you're just starting out, Kartra's simplicity could accelerate your success.

The key is honest assessment of what you're actually building. Don't choose a platform based on features you might need someday—choose based on what will make you successful in the next 12 months.
