GoHighLevel Twilio Setup: Complete Guide (+ A2P Registration 2026)

GoHighLevel Twilio setup involves creating a Twilio account, connecting it to GoHighLevel via API credentials, purchasing phone numbers ($1.15/month), and completing mandatory A2P 10DLC registration ($19 one-time fee). The process requires brand verification with EIN/business documentation, campaign approval through three-layer review (Twilio, DCA1, DCA2), and phone number linking. SMS costs $0.0079 per segment, voice $0.013 per minute. A2P registration prevents message filtering and ensures deliverability. Setup includes configuring SMS webhooks, voice routing, and compliance requirements like privacy policies. LC Phone offers simplified setup but higher costs. Common mistakes include mismatched business information, missing website compliance pages, and incomplete brand documentation. Timeline typically 3-5 business days for approval.

Why Connect Twilio to GoHighLevel?

Twilio integration gives you complete control over your phone communications in GoHighLevel while keeping costs significantly lower than the built-in LC Phone system. You'll pay actual carrier rates instead of marked-up prices, plus get access to Twilio's advanced features and global reach.

The trade-off? Setup complexity. You'll need to handle A2P 10DLC registration, manage your own compliance, and configure everything manually. But for agencies sending thousands of messages monthly, the cost savings are substantial.

Step 1: Creating Your Twilio Account

Head to Twilio.com and create a new account. You'll need to verify your phone number and email address. Twilio provides $15.50 in free trial credits to get started.

Important: Use the same business information you'll later use for A2P registration. Mismatched details between Twilio and your brand registration cause delays and rejections.

Once verified, upgrade to a paid account immediately. Trial accounts have significant limitations on SMS and voice capabilities that prevent proper GoHighLevel integration.

Initial Twilio Configuration

Navigate to your Twilio Console and locate these critical items:

  • Account SID: Your unique account identifier
  • Auth Token: Your API authentication key
  • Phone Numbers section: Where you'll purchase and manage numbers
  • Messaging services: Required for A2P 10DLC compliance

Step 2: Connecting Twilio to GoHighLevel

In your GoHighLevel account, navigate to Settings > Integrations > Twilio. You'll see fields for your Twilio credentials.

Enter your Account SID and Auth Token from the Twilio Console. GoHighLevel will automatically test the connection and confirm if credentials are valid.

Pro tip: Create a separate Twilio subaccount for each GoHighLevel subaccount if you're managing multiple clients. This keeps billing separate and prevents cross-contamination of phone numbers.

Webhook Configuration

GoHighLevel needs to receive incoming messages and call notifications from Twilio. In your Twilio Console, configure these webhook URLs:

  • SMS webhook: Your GoHighLevel domain + /webhook/twilio/sms
  • Voice webhook: Your GoHighLevel domain + /webhook/twilio/voice
  • Status callback: Your GoHighLevel domain + /webhook/twilio/status

These webhooks enable real-time message delivery, read receipts, and call routing through your GoHighLevel workflows.

Step 3: Purchasing Your First Phone Number

In Twilio's Phone Numbers section, click "Buy a number." Filter by your desired area code, features needed (SMS, voice, MMS), and select a number that fits your brand.

Phone numbers cost $1.15 per month regardless of usage. Choose numbers that are easy to remember or match your business location for better trust and recognition.

Number Configuration

Once purchased, configure your number's messaging and voice settings:

  • Messaging webhook: Point to your GoHighLevel SMS webhook
  • Voice webhook: Point to your GoHighLevel voice webhook
  • Messaging service: Leave blank initially (you'll configure this after A2P registration)

Step 4: A2P 10DLC Registration (The Critical Step)

A2P (Application-to-Person) 10DLC registration is mandatory for all business SMS in the US. Without it, your messages get filtered, blocked, or heavily throttled by carriers.

Why A2P Registration Matters

Carriers implemented A2P 10DLC to combat spam and improve deliverability for legitimate businesses. Unregistered numbers face:

  • Message filtering and blocking
  • Severely limited sending speeds (1 message per minute)
  • Higher chance of being marked as spam
  • Potential number suspension

Registration Process

A2P registration involves two steps: Brand verification and Campaign approval.

Brand Registration Requirements

Gather these documents before starting:

  • EIN (Employer Identification Number) or register as Sole Proprietor
  • Legal business name matching your EIN documentation
  • Complete business address (no P.O. boxes)
  • Live website with functioning contact pages
  • Privacy policy and terms of service pages
  • Business phone number and support email
Important: Brand registrations using EINs created within the last 15 days often get rejected. Wait at least 30 days after EIN creation before registering.

Campaign Registration

After brand approval, submit your campaign describing how you'll use SMS:

  • Campaign purpose: Marketing, notifications, customer service, etc.
  • Message samples: Provide 3-5 examples of actual messages you'll send
  • Opt-in process: Describe how customers consent to receive messages
  • Opt-out process: Explain how customers can stop messages

Registration Timeline and Costs

Expect 3-5 business days for complete approval, though some registrations take longer due to the three-layer review process (Twilio, DCA1 by Syniverse, DCA2 by InfoBip).

Costs breakdown:

  • Brand registration: $4 one-time
  • Campaign registration: $15 one-time
  • Monthly campaign fee: $10/month

Total initial cost: $19, plus $10 monthly ongoing.

Step 5: Setting Up SMS

Once A2P registration is approved, create a Messaging Service in your Twilio Console. This service links your registered campaign to your phone numbers.

Messaging Service Configuration

  • Service name: Match your campaign name for consistency
  • Use case: Select the same use case from your campaign registration
  • Phone numbers: Add your purchased numbers to this service
  • Compliance: Link your approved A2P campaign
Pro tip: Enable "Sticky Sender" in your messaging service to ensure customers always receive messages from the same number, improving conversation continuity.

SMS Pricing

Twilio charges $0.0079 per SMS segment (160 characters). Messages longer than 160 characters split into multiple segments, each charged separately. MMS costs $0.02 per message.

For detailed cost planning, use our SMS cost estimator tool to calculate monthly expenses based on your message volume.

Step 6: Setting Up Voice and Calling

Voice setup is simpler than SMS since it doesn't require A2P registration. Configure your phone numbers for inbound and outbound calling.

Voice Configuration

In your phone number settings, configure:

  • Voice webhook: GoHighLevel's voice webhook URL
  • Call recording: Enable if needed for compliance or quality assurance
  • Caller ID: Set your business name for outbound calls
  • Voice routing: Configure forwarding, voicemail, and business hours

Voice Pricing

Voice calls cost $0.013 per minute for US calls. International rates vary by country. Recording adds $0.0025 per minute when enabled.

Compared to traditional phone systems, Twilio's voice costs are competitive, especially for businesses making hundreds of calls monthly.

Cost Optimization Tips

Twilio's pay-as-you-go model rewards efficient usage. Here's how to minimize costs:

SMS Optimization

  • Message length: Keep messages under 160 characters to avoid multi-segment charges
  • Smart scheduling: Use GoHighLevel's scheduling to avoid sending messages at peak pricing times
  • Audience segmentation: Only send relevant messages to engaged subscribers
  • Template reuse: Create efficient message templates that convey maximum information in minimal characters

Voice Optimization

  • Call routing: Use intelligent routing to minimize call duration
  • Voicemail detection: Automatically hang up on voicemails to avoid per-minute charges
  • Business hours: Configure calling windows to improve answer rates
Pro tip: Monitor your Twilio usage dashboard monthly. Set up billing alerts to catch unexpected usage spikes before they impact your budget.

Common Twilio Setup Mistakes

Avoid these frequent errors that cause setup delays and compliance issues:

Documentation Mismatches

The most common mistake is inconsistent business information across platforms. Your Twilio account, A2P registration, and website must show identical:

  • Business name and address
  • Contact phone numbers
  • Support email addresses
  • Legal entity structure

Website Compliance Gaps

Carriers manually review your website during A2P registration. Common rejection reasons:

  • Missing or incomplete privacy policy
  • No terms of service page
  • Broken contact form or email links
  • Website under construction or placeholder content

Message Sample Problems

Campaign rejections often stem from poor message samples. Avoid:

  • Generic placeholder text
  • Missing opt-out instructions
  • Misleading or spammy language
  • Samples that don't match your stated campaign purpose
Important: Test your complete setup with a small message volume before launching major campaigns. This reveals configuration issues without impacting large subscriber lists.

Webhook Configuration Errors

Incorrect webhook URLs break message delivery and call routing. Double-check:

  • URLs include your correct GoHighLevel subdomain
  • HTTPS protocol (required for security)
  • Webhook paths match GoHighLevel's requirements exactly
  • Status callbacks are configured for delivery notifications

Advanced Configuration for Power Users

Once basic setup is complete, consider these advanced configurations for improved performance and compliance:

Multiple Messaging Services

Create separate messaging services for different campaign types (marketing vs. transactional) to improve deliverability and tracking.

Smart Encoding

Configure character encoding to handle emoji and special characters efficiently, preventing unexpected multi-segment charges.

Delivery Optimization

Use Twilio's delivery optimization features to automatically retry failed messages and improve overall delivery rates.
