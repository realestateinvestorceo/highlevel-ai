Building the 60-Second Response Workflow in GoHighLevel

GoHighLevel's Workflow Builder transforms speed-to-lead from a manual scramble into a systematic advantage. Here's how to build an instant response system that captures leads while competitors are still checking their phones.

Setting Up the Trigger

Navigate to Automation → Workflows and create a new workflow. Your trigger options determine when the instant response fires:

  • Contact Tag Added: Best for lead magnets and form submissions
  • Form Submission: Direct trigger from landing pages
  • Missed Call: Captures leads who call but don't connect
  • Opportunity Created: Triggers when leads enter your sales pipeline

For maximum coverage, I recommend starting with "Contact Tag Added" using a tag like "New Lead" that gets applied across all lead sources.

The Instant SMS Response

Add an SMS action immediately after your trigger. Set the timing to "0 minutes" for instant delivery. The key is personalization using custom values:

Pro tip: Use the contact's first name and reference their specific inquiry using custom fields. Generic responses get ignored—personalized ones get replies.

Business Hours Controls

Add a "Wait" step with business hour restrictions (e.g., 8 AM - 6 PM, Monday-Friday). Leads captured outside business hours get queued and sent first thing the next morning, preventing 2 AM text messages that damage your brand.

Response Detection and Follow-Up

Enable "Stop on Response" to pause the workflow when leads reply. Then add a 15-minute wait before the next touchpoint—enough time for human response without losing momentum.

Your complete workflow structure should look like this:

  1. Trigger: Contact Tag Added ("New Lead")
  2. Wait: If outside business hours
  3. SMS: Instant personalized response
  4. Wait: 15 minutes (stop on response)
  5. Email: Follow-up with additional value
  6. Wait: 2 hours (stop on response)
  7. SMS: Second touchpoint
  8. Continue sequence...

High-Converting SMS Template for Instant Response

Your instant SMS needs to accomplish three things: acknowledge their inquiry, provide immediate value, and create urgency for next steps. Here's a proven template:

Hi {{contact.first_name}}, thanks for your interest in {{custom_values.service}}! 🎯 I just received your inquiry and have 2 spots available this week for {{custom_values.service}}. I'll call you in the next 10 minutes to discuss your project. If now isn't good, reply with a better time. - [Your Name], [Company] P.S. Check your email for our free [relevant guide/checklist]

This template works because it:

  • Uses their name and references their specific inquiry
  • Creates scarcity with "2 spots available"
  • Sets clear expectations for next contact
  • Provides an easy way to reschedule
  • Mentions additional value in email
Important: Always follow through on promises made in your instant response. If you say you'll call in 10 minutes, call in 10 minutes. Credibility is built or destroyed in these first interactions.

Email Template for Instant Response

The Multi-Channel Response Cascade

The most effective speed-to-lead strategy doesn't rely on a single channel—it cascades across multiple touchpoints until you achieve engagement.

The 4-Touch Cascade System

Touch 1 (0 minutes): Instant SMS
Immediate acknowledgment with clear next steps and timeline for follow-up.

Touch 2 (2 minutes): Instant Email
Value-add content, social proof, and reinforcement of SMS message.

Touch 3 (5 minutes): AI Chatbot Activation
Proactive chat invitation if no response to SMS/email, offering immediate assistance.

Touch 4 (10 minutes): Voicemail Drop
Pre-recorded personalized voicemail referencing their inquiry and previous touchpoints.

This cascade ensures 100% of leads receive immediate attention across their preferred communication channels. Most competitors are lucky to respond within an hour—you're covering all bases within 10 minutes.
