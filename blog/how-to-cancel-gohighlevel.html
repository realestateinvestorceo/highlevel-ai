How to Cancel Your GoHighLevel Subscription (Step-by-Step)

I get it. You're frustrated with GoHighLevel and ready to pull the plug. Maybe it's too expensive, too complicated, or just not working for your business. Whatever brought you here, I'll walk you through the exact cancellation process — and share some alternatives that might save you time and money.

Important: Before you cancel, export ALL your data. Once you confirm cancellation, everything gets permanently deleted — contacts, funnels, campaigns, everything.

Here's exactly how to cancel your GoHighLevel subscription:

Step 1: Switch to Your Agency Dashboard

Make sure you're in your agency view, not a sub-account. Click your account name in the top-left corner and select your main agency if you're not already there. You can't cancel from a sub-account — only from the main agency dashboard.

Step 2: Navigate to Billing Settings

From your main dashboard:

  1. Click Settings in the bottom-left corner
  2. Select Billing (it's the fourth option down)
  3. Look for the "Want to modify/cancel your subscription?" link

Step 3: Start the Cancellation Process

Click "Show more" to expand your options, then select "Cancel Plan" or "Cancel subscription."

Step 4: Provide Cancellation Details

GoHighLevel will ask you to:

  • Choose a reason for cancellation (too expensive, too complicated, etc.)
  • Acknowledge the data loss warning
  • Confirm "I am sure I want to cancel"
  • Provide feedback (minimum 30 words required)
  • Enter your password to confirm

Once you complete these steps, your cancellation is processed. Your subscription remains active until the end of your current billing period, but some features may lose access immediately.

Pro tip: You can also visit gohighlevel.com/modify-subscription directly to access modification options.

What Happens to Your Data When You Cancel

This is where GoHighLevel gets harsh: everything gets deleted permanently. We're talking about:

  • All your contacts and their conversation history
  • Funnels, websites, and landing pages
  • Automation workflows and campaigns
  • Call recordings and voicemails
  • Reports and analytics data
  • Custom fields and pipeline configurations

There's no grace period, no "are you really sure?" safety net. Once your billing period ends, it's gone forever.

Important: If you have any doubt about canceling, export your data first or consider pausing your account instead.

How to Export Your Contacts and Data

Before you hit that final cancel button, save what you can. GoHighLevel doesn't make this obvious, but you can export most of your important data:

Exporting Contacts

  1. Go to the Contacts tab in your dashboard
  2. Click the three dots menu (⋯) in the top right
  3. Select "Export Contacts"
  4. Choose your export format (CSV is most compatible)
  5. Download the file immediately

Exporting Other Data

  • Campaign reports: Go to each campaign and use the export function in the reporting section
  • Call recordings: Download individually from the conversations tab
  • Funnels and websites: Take screenshots and copy any custom code or content
  • Automation workflows: Screenshot your workflow diagrams for reference
Pro tip: Connect Google Docs or other tools to save any documents or templates you've created in GoHighLevel.

Can You Downgrade Instead of Canceling?

Absolutely, and this might be the smarter move. Instead of going nuclear with a full cancellation, you can downgrade your plan through the same billing page.

Your downgrade options include:

  • Unlimited ($297/month) → Starter ($97/month): Keep core CRM and automation features
  • Reduce sub-accounts: Lower your monthly cost by removing unused sub-accounts
  • Pause specific add-ons: Keep the platform but drop expensive extras like phone numbers or integrations

The $97 Starter plan includes:

  • Up to 1,000 contacts
  • Unlimited funnels and websites
  • Email and SMS marketing
  • Basic automation workflows
  • Pipeline management

For many small businesses, this covers 80% of what they actually use.

Can You Pause Your GoHighLevel Account?

GoHighLevel doesn't offer a traditional "pause" feature, but you have a few alternatives:

The Starter Plan Pause Strategy

Downgrade to the $97 Starter plan temporarily. This keeps your data safe while dramatically reducing costs. You can upgrade back to Unlimited anytime without losing your setup.

Transfer Sub-Accounts

If you're an agency, you can transfer your sub-accounts to another GoHighLevel agency. This keeps client data intact while removing your monthly costs. The receiving agency takes over billing and management.

Annual Payment Break

Switch to annual billing for a discount, then you won't see another charge for 12 months. This isn't exactly pausing, but it removes the monthly payment stress.

3 Things to Try Before Canceling GoHighLevel

Before you pull the trigger, consider whether these common issues are actually fixable:

1. Feature Overwhelm

GoHighLevel has hundreds of features, and most users only need about 10-15 of them. Instead of canceling, try this:

  • Hide unused menu items in Settings > Company Settings
  • Focus on just CRM + Email marketing for 30 days
  • Add one new feature per week, not all at once
Pro tip: Check out our GoHighLevel setup guide for a simplified 30-day implementation plan.

2. Integration Problems

If GoHighLevel isn't talking to your other tools, the problem might be simpler than you think:

  • Use Zapier or Make.com instead of native integrations
  • Check if you're on the right plan (some integrations require Unlimited)
  • Try GoHighLevel's API documentation for custom connections

3. Workflow Complexity

Those automation workflows can get messy fast. Instead of abandoning ship:

  • Start with simple 2-step automations
  • Use GoHighLevel's pre-built workflow templates
  • Test each step individually before building complex sequences

When Canceling IS the Right Move

Let me be honest: GoHighLevel isn't for everyone. After 6+ years of using it, I've seen plenty of businesses where canceling makes perfect sense.

You Should Cancel If:

You're getting fewer than 10 leads per month: GoHighLevel's power comes from managing lots of contacts and automating repetitive tasks. If you're not generating consistent leads, you're paying for overkill.

You don't use automation: If you prefer handling everything manually and don't want automated follow-ups, email sequences, or workflow triggers, GoHighLevel's core value proposition doesn't apply to you.

You're ecommerce-only: GoHighLevel works best for service businesses with longer sales cycles. If you're running a Shopify store and just need basic email marketing, platforms like Klaviyo or Mailchimp make more sense.

You need simple contact management only: If all you want is a basic contact database without marketing automation, you're overpaying. A simple CRM like Pipedrive or even Google Contacts might be enough.

Your team won't use it: I've seen agencies spend months trying to force their team to adopt GoHighLevel. If your staff actively resists using it after 3+ months of training, the productivity hit isn't worth the monthly cost.

Pro tip: Before canceling for team adoption issues, try our GoHighLevel training resources or consider bringing in a consultant for proper implementation.

The $97 Starter Plan Compromise

If you're on the fence about canceling, the Starter plan might be your middle ground. At $97/month, it's less than half the cost of the Unlimited plan but keeps most of the features small businesses actually use.

What you keep with Starter:

  • Full CRM with up to 1,000 contacts
  • Unlimited email marketing
  • SMS marketing (pay per message)
  • Landing pages and funnels
  • Basic automation workflows
  • Pipeline management
  • Appointment scheduling

What you lose:

  • Sub-accounts (agency features)
  • Advanced automation triggers
  • White-label options
  • Some third-party integrations
  • Advanced reporting

For solo entrepreneurs and small teams, this is often the sweet spot between functionality and cost.

"I downgraded to Starter when I realized I was only using GoHighLevel for email marketing and basic CRM. Saved $200/month and honestly didn't miss any of the advanced features." — Sarah K., Marketing Consultant

What to Do After Canceling

If you've decided to cancel and move on, here's your migration checklist:

Immediate Steps

  • Import your exported contacts into your new system
  • Update any website forms to point to your new CRM
  • Redirect any GoHighLevel landing page URLs
  • Cancel any add-on services (phone numbers, domains)

Alternative Platforms to Consider

  • For basic CRM: Pipedrive, HubSpot Free, or Zoho
  • For email marketing: Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or ActiveCampaign
  • For landing pages: Leadpages, ClickFunnels, or Unbounce
  • For automation: ActiveCampaign or Keap
Important: No single platform replaces all of GoHighLevel's features. You'll likely need 2-3 different tools, which can end up costing more than GoHighLevel's monthly fee.

Bottom Line

Canceling GoHighLevel is straightforward but permanent. Once you confirm that cancellation, your data is gone forever — no takebacks, no recovery options.

Before you cancel, honestly assess whether the problem is with the platform or with how you're using it. Many "GoHighLevel problems" are actually implementation issues that can be fixed with better training or simplified workflows.

If you're overwhelmed by features, try the $97 Starter plan instead of canceling completely. If you're not generating enough leads to justify any CRM cost, canceling might be the right business decision.

Either way, make sure you export your data first. That contact list and conversation history took months or years to build — don't lose it because you forgot to click "export" before hitting "cancel."
