MIGRATION

Migrating from ActiveCampaign to GoHighLevel

Step 1: Export Your Contacts

In ActiveCampaign, navigate to Contacts and use the export function to download your entire contact database as a CSV file. This will include custom fields, tags, and list assignments. Make sure to export any deal data separately from the CRM section if you are using ActiveCampaign's deals feature.

Step 2: Export Your Automations

ActiveCampaign does not offer a direct automation export in a portable format. Before canceling, document each automation by taking screenshots or writing down the trigger conditions, actions, wait steps, and conditional branches. This documentation will serve as your blueprint when rebuilding automations in GoHighLevel's workflow builder.

Step 3: Import into GoHighLevel

In GoHighLevel, use the CSV import tool to bring in your contacts. Map your ActiveCampaign custom fields to GoHighLevel custom fields during the import process. Tags can be imported directly. After import, verify that contact data, tags, and custom field values transferred correctly.

Step 4: Rebuild Automations

Recreate your ActiveCampaign automations using GoHighLevel's visual workflow builder. While the interface is different, the drag-and-drop logic is similar. The key advantage is that you can now add SMS messages, phone calls, voicemail drops, and AI chatbot triggers directly within the same automation — channels that required separate tools alongside ActiveCampaign.

Timeline

Most businesses complete the migration within one to two weeks depending on the number of automations and the complexity of their setup. We recommend running both platforms in parallel for at least one week before fully transitioning to catch any gaps.
