CRITICISM

What Real Users Complain About

The top five complaint themes that emerge across all three review platforms.

  1. The learning curve is real and significant.This is the number one complaint across every platform. GoHighLevel does so much that new users often feel overwhelmed trying to learn the CRM, funnel builder, workflow automation, and messaging tools all at once. Most reviewers say it takes 2–4 weeks to feel comfortable and several months to master the platform.[1][2]
  2. Twilio costs add up and are not transparent upfront. SMS and voice features require a connected Twilio account, and the per-message costs are separate from GoHighLevel's subscription. Several reviewers say they were surprised by these additional charges after signing up.[2]
  3. The interface can feel cluttered and overwhelming. Users coming from simpler single-purpose tools often find GoHighLevel's interface confusing at first. The navigation has many menu items, and some settings are buried in sub-menus that are not intuitive to find.[1]
  4. Mobile app does not match the desktop experience. While GoHighLevel has a mobile app, reviewers consistently note that it lacks feature parity with the desktop version. Key workflow and funnel building features are either missing or harder to use on mobile.[2]
  5. Email deliverability requires technical setup. Unlike some email marketing platforms that handle deliverability automatically, GoHighLevel requires users to configure SPF, DKIM, and sending domain records properly. Users who skip this step report poor email deliverability.[2]
PERSONAL EXPERIENCE

My Take as a 6-Year User

An honest perspective from someone who has used GoHighLevel since 2019.

Having used GoHighLevel since 2019 on the SaaS Pro plan, here is how I would characterize the reviews: they are mostly accurate.

The praise is earned.The cost savings are real. Before GoHighLevel, I was paying for ClickFunnels, ActiveCampaign, Calendly, CallRail, and a separate CRM. That stack ran me over $400 per month. GoHighLevel replaced all of them. The white-label SaaS mode has also been a legitimate revenue generator — I resell the platform to clients under my own brand, which covers my subscription cost and then some.

The complaints are valid, too. The learning curve is the single biggest barrier I see with new users. When I started in 2019, the platform had far fewer features, so I was able to learn incrementally as things were added. Someone starting today faces a much larger feature set all at once. I would estimate 3–4 weeks of dedicated use before things start to click, and 2–3 months before you feel like you really know the platform.

The Twilio cost issue is legitimate but manageable. Yes, SMS and voice costs are separate. But in practice, my Twilio bill runs $30–$60 per month depending on volume — far less than what I was paying for a separate SMS tool and phone system before. The key is to understand these costs exist before you sign up so there are no surprises.

The platform has improved dramatically.The GoHighLevel I use today barely resembles what I signed up for in 2019. The AI conversation bot, voice agent, content AI, membership sites, and dozens of other features have been added since then. The development velocity is genuinely impressive — the team ships meaningful updates almost every week.

My honest recommendation: If you are an agency, consultant, or business owner currently juggling three or more marketing tools, GoHighLevel is worth the 14-day trial. The learning curve is real, but the long-term cost savings and operational simplification make it worthwhile.
