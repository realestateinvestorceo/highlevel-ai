7 GoHighLevel Cons: The Real Downsides

1. Steep Learning Curve (2-4 Weeks to Be Comfortable)

GoHighLevel's biggest weakness is its complexity. Getting functionally proficient takes 2-4 weeks of daily use, and reaching genuine confidence requires 6-8 weeks. Settings are scattered across multiple menus, and basic tasks often require non-intuitive navigation.

For example, setting up proper email authentication requires configuring DNS records in your domain registrar, then adjusting settings in three different GoHighLevel menus. Miss one step, and your emails go to spam—but the platform doesn't clearly guide you through the process.

This learning curve isn't just about features—it's about understanding GoHighLevel's unique logic and terminology. Concepts like "snapshots," "sub-accounts," and "opportunities" work differently than other platforms, requiring mental adjustment even for experienced marketers.

Important: Budget 20-30 hours for initial learning, plus ongoing time for advanced features. This isn't a weekend project—it's a significant platform migration that requires dedicated effort.

2. UI Can Feel Cluttered and Overwhelming

GoHighLevel's interface suffers from feature bloat. The left navigation menu includes 20+ options, each with multiple sub-menus and configuration screens. Finding specific settings often requires clicking through 4-5 screens, and the search function doesn't always surface relevant results.

The mobile app compounds this problem—screens that feel cramped on desktop become nearly unusable on phones. Critical functions like contact management and opportunity updates are possible on mobile, but the experience is frustrating compared to dedicated mobile-first apps.

New users frequently get lost trying to accomplish basic tasks like creating email templates or setting up automation triggers. The abundance of options creates decision paralysis rather than empowerment.

3. Twilio/Mailgun Add-On Costs Aren't Obvious Upfront

GoHighLevel's pricing appears straightforward until you discover the hidden costs for SMS and email delivery. The platform uses Twilio for SMS and Mailgun for email, both requiring separate billing relationships with usage-based pricing.

SMS costs typically run $0.01-0.03 per message, which adds up quickly for active campaigns. A client sending 10,000 SMS messages monthly pays an additional $100-300 beyond their GoHighLevel subscription. Email costs are lower but still significant for high-volume senders.

These costs aren't clearly disclosed during signup, leading to surprise bills in month two. Budgeting becomes complicated because usage varies based on campaign activity, making it difficult to predict monthly expenses.

For more details on hidden costs, check out our comprehensive GoHighLevel pricing breakdown.

4. Mobile App Is Limited Compared to Desktop

While GoHighLevel offers mobile apps for iOS and Android, they feel like afterthoughts compared to the desktop experience. Critical functions like workflow editing, funnel building, and detailed reporting are either missing or severely limited on mobile.

The mobile app works adequately for basic tasks like responding to leads, checking notifications, and updating contact records. However, any serious configuration work requires switching to desktop, making it impossible to manage campaigns effectively while traveling.

Compared to mobile-first platforms like many social media management tools, GoHighLevel's mobile experience feels clunky and incomplete.

5. Customer Support Response Times Vary

GoHighLevel's support quality is inconsistent. During business hours, chat support typically responds within 10-15 minutes with helpful answers. However, evenings, weekends, and busy periods can result in much longer wait times and less experienced support agents.

The knowledge base is comprehensive but not always up-to-date with recent feature changes. Video tutorials help, but they often skip important setup details that cause implementation problems.

For complex technical issues, expect to escalate through multiple support tiers before reaching someone who can actually solve the problem. This process can take 24-48 hours for non-urgent issues.

6. Some Features Feel Half-Baked (Courses, Social Planner)

GoHighLevel's rapid development cycle sometimes prioritizes launching features over perfecting them. The course creation tool, for example, handles basic video hosting and student management but lacks advanced features like certificates, quizzes, or progress tracking that dedicated platforms offer.

The social media planner can schedule posts to major platforms but doesn't include content creation tools, advanced analytics, or team collaboration features that make dedicated tools valuable.

These "good enough" implementations work for basic needs but fall short if you require sophisticated functionality. You'll likely still need specialized tools for advanced use cases.

7. Aggressive Affiliate Ecosystem Creates Biased Reviews

GoHighLevel's affiliate program pays generous commissions, creating an ecosystem of biased reviews and recommendations. Many "independent" reviews are actually affiliate marketing disguised as objective analysis.

This makes it difficult to find honest assessments of the platform's limitations. Most content focuses on benefits while glossing over significant drawbacks like the learning curve, hidden costs, and feature limitations.

The affiliate incentive structure also means you'll receive aggressive follow-up marketing after signing up, with partners pitching expensive training courses and implementation services.

Important: Be skeptical of overwhelmingly positive reviews, especially those with affiliate links. Look for balanced assessments that discuss both strengths and weaknesses honestly.

Who Should (and Shouldn't) Use GoHighLevel

It's less suitable for small businesses seeking simple, out-of-the-box solutions or companies requiring best-in-class email deliverability. If you need a CRM that works perfectly on day one without configuration, consider alternatives like HubSpot or Pipedrive.
