Budget Planning

Hidden Costs to Budget For

The subscription price is not your full monthly spend. Here are the usage-based costs most people overlook.

GoHighLevel acts as a platform layer, but it relies on third-party services for SMS delivery, voice calls, and high-volume email sending. These costs are usage-based and billed separately from your subscription. The amounts vary widely depending on your message volume, but here is what to expect.

Twilio (SMS & Voice)

GoHighLevel uses Twilio as its primary SMS and voice infrastructure provider. You create a Twilio account, connect it to GoHighLevel, and pay Twilio directly based on usage. SMS messages cost approximately $0.0079 per segment for outbound messages in the US.[2] Inbound SMS is approximately $0.0079 per segment as well. Voice calls cost around $0.013 per minute for outbound and $0.0085 per minute for inbound.[3] A local phone number runs about $1.15 per month.

GoHighLevel also offers LC Phone, its own built-in telephony option that simplifies setup by removing the need to manage a separate Twilio account. LC Phone pricing is slightly higher per message than raw Twilio rates, but the convenience may be worth it for users who prefer a single dashboard.

Mailgun (Email Sending)

For email marketing at scale, GoHighLevel integrates with Mailgun as its recommended email delivery provider. Mailgun offers a free tier of 100 emails per day, but most businesses will need a paid plan. Mailgun pricing starts at approximately $0.80 per 1,000 emails on the Foundation plan.[4] If you send 10,000 emails per month, that adds roughly $8 to your bill. At 50,000 emails per month, expect around $35.

A2P 10DLC Registration

If you send business SMS in the United States, carriers now require A2P 10DLC (Application-to-Person 10 Digit Long Code) registration. This involves a one-time brand registration fee of approximately $4 and a campaign registration fee of $15.[2] There is also a recurring monthly fee of around $2 per registered campaign. This is a carrier-level requirement, not a GoHighLevel-specific charge, and it applies to any platform that sends business text messages.

Premium Workflow Actions

Most workflow actions in GoHighLevel are included with your plan. However, certain premium actions — such as AI-powered content generation within workflows, advanced conditional logic, and some third-party triggers — may carry small per-execution fees. These are typically fractions of a cent per action and only affect high-volume users running thousands of automated sequences per month.

Decision Guide

Which Plan Should You Choose?

The right plan depends on your business model, not just your budget.

Choose Starter ($97/mo) If...

You are a solo business owner, freelancer, or small team that manages marketing for a single business. The Starter plan gives you the full platform — CRM, funnels, email and SMS, AI bot, scheduling, and workflow automations — without the features designed for multi-client agencies. This is the right starting point if you want to test GoHighLevel before committing to a higher tier, or if you simply do not need sub-accounts for multiple clients.[1]

Choose Unlimited ($297/mo) If...

You are a marketing agency, consultant, or service provider managing multiple clients. The Unlimited plan removes the sub-account cap, gives you unlimited contacts, adds API access for custom integrations, and includes the branded desktop app. Most agencies start here because the per-client economics work out well: at $297/mo for the platform plus modest add-on costs, you can profitably serve five, ten, or fifty clients from a single dashboard. If you charge clients $200–$500/mo for your marketing services, this plan pays for itself with just one or two clients.[1]

Choose SaaS Pro ($497/mo) If...

You want to white-label GoHighLevel and sell it as your own branded software product. The SaaS Pro plan lets you rebrand the entire platform — custom domain, login page, logo, colors — and resell it to your clients at whatever price you set using built-in subscription billing. Many agencies charge their clients $297–$997/mo for access to their white-labeled platform, creating a recurring SaaS revenue stream. This plan makes sense only if you want to move beyond service delivery into building a software business.[1]

Not Sure? Start With Starter

All plans include a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. If you are uncertain, start with the Starter plan. You can upgrade at any time from your billing settings, and the transition is seamless — your data, workflows, and configurations carry over. There is no penalty for starting small and scaling up.
