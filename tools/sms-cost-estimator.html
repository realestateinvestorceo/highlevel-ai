SMS Segment Character Reference

SMS messages are split into segments based on character count. Each segment is billed separately, so keeping messages short saves money.

Characters Segments Cost (US)
1 – 1601$0.0079
161 – 3062$0.0158
307 – 4593$0.0237
460 – 6124$0.0316
613 – 7655$0.0395

Note: When a message exceeds 160 characters, the segment size drops to 153 characters per segment (7 characters are used for the concatenation header). Unicode characters (emojis, special characters) reduce the segment limit to 70 characters for the first segment and 67 for subsequent segments.

Tips to Reduce Your SMS Costs

5 Ways to Lower Your Twilio Bill
  • Keep messages under 160 characters. Every extra segment doubles (or triples) the per-message cost. Edit ruthlessly.
  • Avoid MMS when possible. MMS costs $0.0200 per message vs $0.0079 for SMS. Only use MMS when the image is genuinely necessary.
  • Use email for long content. If your message needs more than 2–3 sentences, send an email instead. GoHighLevel lets you mix channels in workflows.
  • Avoid emojis and special characters. Unicode characters reduce your segment capacity from 160 to 70 characters, drastically increasing segment count.
  • Segment your audience. Send SMS only to engaged contacts. Blasting your entire list wastes money on contacts who will never respond.

A2P 10DLC Registration

Required for US Business Texting

A2P 10DLC (Application-to-Person 10-Digit Long Code) registration is required by US carriers for all business SMS. Without it, your messages may be filtered, delayed, or blocked entirely.

Cost: $19 one-time registration fee through GoHighLevel's Twilio integration.

What it covers: Registers your business and use case with The Campaign Registry (TCR), allowing carriers to verify that your messages are legitimate business communications.

Timeline: Approval typically takes 1–5 business days. You can still send SMS during this period, but throughput may be limited.
