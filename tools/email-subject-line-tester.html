    5 Email Subject Line Best Practices

    Writing subject lines that get opened is both an art and a science. Here are five data-backed tips to consistently boost your open rates.

    1. Keep it between 30–50 characters. Most mobile email clients truncate subject lines at 35 characters. Desktop clients show around 60. Aim for the sweet spot where your full message displays on both.
    2. Use one power word. Words like "exclusive," "proven," and "essential" trigger curiosity. Don't overdo it—one strong power word is more effective than stacking three.
    3. Add a number or statistic. Subject lines containing a number see roughly 15% higher open rates. Specificity builds credibility (e.g., "5 ways" or "23% increase").
    4. Create urgency without being spammy. Phrases like "ending tonight" or "last chance" work. All-caps "ACT NOW!!!" ends up in spam. The line between urgency and spam is subtlety.
    5. A/B test everything. Even small changes in wording can swing open rates by 10–20%. Send two variants to a small segment, then roll out the winner to your full list.
