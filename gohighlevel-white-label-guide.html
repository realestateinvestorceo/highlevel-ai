What Is White-Label SaaS Mode?

GoHighLevel's SaaS Pro plan lets marketing agencies and entrepreneurs rebrand the entire GoHighLevel platform as their own proprietary software. Every element your clients interact with carries your brand: your custom domain, your logo, your color scheme, and your app name. Clients log in to your domain, use your branded mobile app, and never encounter the name "GoHighLevel" anywhere in their experience.[1]

This is not a simple logo swap. White-label mode gives you full control over the desktop web application, a branded iOS and Android mobile app published under your name in the app stores, custom email sending domains, and a branded login portal. You also get built-in Stripe billing integration so you can create subscription plans, charge clients automatically, and manage recurring revenue — all from within your white-labeled dashboard.[2]

The value proposition is straightforward: instead of spending $100,000–$500,000+ and 12–18 months building a CRM or marketing automation platform from scratch, you pay $497/mo and get a fully functional, battle-tested platform on day one. GoHighLevel handles the infrastructure, updates, server maintenance, and feature development. You focus on sales, client success, and building your brand.

No other CRM or marketing automation platform offers this level of white-labeling at a comparable price point. Competitors like HubSpot, ActiveCampaign, and Salesforce do not offer white-label reselling at all. Vendasta and similar white-label platforms exist but typically charge significantly more and offer a narrower feature set.[3]

Step-by-Step Setup: Launch Your White-Label SaaS

Follow these eight steps to go from zero to a fully branded SaaS platform ready to onboard clients. Most agencies complete this process in 1–2 weeks.

  1. Sign up for the GoHighLevel SaaS Pro plan. Start with the 14-day free trial to test the full white-label experience before committing to $497/mo. You get access to all SaaS Pro features during the trial.[1]
  2. Choose your brand name and register your domain. Pick a professional brand name for your SaaS product (e.g., "AgencyOS," "ClientHub," or a niche-specific name like "DentalFlow CRM"). Register a domain and set up DNS records to point to your white-labeled GoHighLevel instance.
  3. Configure white-label settings. Upload your logo, set your brand colors, add your favicon, and apply any custom CSS to match your brand identity. This controls how the platform looks when clients log in.[1]
  4. Set up your custom domain for the client-facing app. Map your domain (e.g., app.yoursaas.com) to GoHighLevel so clients access your branded URL — not a GoHighLevel subdomain. Configure SSL certificates for secure access.
  5. Configure Twilio and Mailgun for messaging. Create accounts with Twilio (SMS/voice) and Mailgun (email), then connect them to your GoHighLevel account using API keys. These third-party integrations handle the actual message delivery for your clients.
  6. Create your pricing tiers and subscription plans. Use GoHighLevel's SaaS Configurator to build your pricing plans. Connect Stripe for payment processing. Define which features each tier includes — for example, a $97/mo starter plan with basic CRM, a $197/mo growth plan with automation, and a $297/mo pro plan with everything included.
  7. Build your first client sub-account as a template. Create a "snapshot" — a pre-built sub-account template with workflows, funnels, email templates, and pipeline stages already configured. When you onboard a new client, you deploy this snapshot to give them a ready-to-use account in minutes instead of hours.
  8. Launch and onboard your first client. Create a sub-account for your first client, deploy your snapshot template, customize it for their business, and walk them through the platform. Use this first onboarding to refine your process before scaling.

Who Should Use White-Label Mode?

White-labeling GoHighLevel is not for everyone. It works best for businesses and individuals who want to build recurring revenue by providing software and services to clients. Here are the ideal use cases:

Marketing Agencies

Agencies that already manage client marketing can add a SaaS revenue stream on top of their service fees. Instead of just running ads and building funnels for clients, you sell them the platform itself. This creates sticky MRR that continues even if a client pauses their service contract. Many agencies report that SaaS revenue eventually exceeds their service revenue.[4]

Freelancers Building a SaaS Side Business

Freelance marketers, web designers, and consultants can use white-label GoHighLevel to transition from trading time for money to building a scalable product business. Start with a few clients on the side and grow it into a full-time SaaS company.

Coaches and Consultants

Business coaches and consultants who advise clients on marketing and operations can provide their own branded platform as part of their coaching packages. This increases the perceived value of your coaching and creates a tool-based lock-in that reduces client churn.

Industry-Specific Software Companies

Entrepreneurs targeting a specific vertical — dental practices, real estate agents, fitness studios, home services, law firms — can build a niche CRM product on top of GoHighLevel. Pre-configure workflows, templates, and pipelines specific to that industry, then sell it as a purpose-built solution. Niche SaaS products often command higher prices because they solve industry-specific problems out of the box.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After years of observing the GoHighLevel reseller community, these are the most frequent mistakes that cause white-label SaaS businesses to stall or fail:

  • Pricing too low. Charging under $97/mo does not cover the support time you will spend helping clients. Factor in onboarding calls, troubleshooting, and ongoing questions. Most successful resellers charge $197–$297/mo minimum.[4]
  • Not investing in onboarding. Clients who do not get proper setup and training churn within 60 days. Build a structured onboarding process: kickoff call, account configuration, training videos, and a 30-day check-in. The upfront time investment pays off in retention.
  • Trying to be everything to everyone. Pick a niche. "CRM for everyone" is impossible to market. "Booking and review automation for dental practices" is specific, sellable, and allows you to build templates that work out of the box. Niche resellers consistently outperform generalists.
  • Skipping Twilio setup and relying on LC Phone. GoHighLevel's built-in LC Phone works for getting started, but at scale, the per-message costs are higher than using your own Twilio account. Set up Twilio from day one to keep your messaging costs predictable and lower.[3]
  • Not creating snapshot templates. If you manually set up every new client account from scratch, onboarding takes hours instead of minutes. Build snapshot templates with pre-configured workflows, pipelines, email sequences, and funnel pages. Deploy a snapshot, customize the details, and your client is live in under an hour.
