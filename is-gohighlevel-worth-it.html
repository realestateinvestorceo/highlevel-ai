Who Should NOT Buy GoHighLevel

I've seen too many people buy GHL when they shouldn't have. Here's who should avoid it:

Ecommerce-Only Businesses

GoHighLevel's ecommerce features are basic at best. If you're primarily selling physical products, Shopify + Klaviyo will serve you better. GHL doesn't integrate well with inventory management or complex product catalogs.

Solo Businesses Under $5K/Month Revenue

The learning curve isn't worth it if you're just starting out. You'll spend more time learning the platform than growing your business. Stick with simple tools like Mailchimp + Calendly until you hit consistent $5K months.

Businesses Needing Best-in-Class Single Features

If you need the absolute best email deliverability, go with ConvertKit or ActiveCampaign. For advanced CRM features, HubSpot wins. GHL is "good enough" at everything but exceptional at nothing except consolidation.

WordPress-Heavy Operations

If your entire business revolves around WordPress sites, GHL's website builder feels like a downgrade. The integration isn't seamless, and you'll fight the system.

Important: Don't buy GoHighLevel just because it's popular. The complexity requires genuine business justification, not FOMO.

The 3 Things I'd Change About GoHighLevel

After 6 years, here's what still frustrates me:

1. The User Interface Needs Work

It feels like software designed by developers, not UX designers. Simple tasks often require multiple clicks, and the navigation isn't intuitive. ClickFunnels 2.0 spoiled me with clean design.

2. Email Deliverability Isn't Plug-and-Play

You'll need to set up DKIM, SPF, and DMARC records manually. Most competitors handle this automatically. Budget 2-3 hours for proper email authentication setup, or your emails will land in spam.

3. Feature Rollouts Are Inconsistent

GoHighLevel adds features constantly (which is great), but they're often half-baked initially. The AI features in 2024-2026 felt rushed. I prefer fewer features that work perfectly over many features that work okay.

Important: These aren't dealbreakers, but they're why I don't recommend GHL for people who need simplicity over power.
