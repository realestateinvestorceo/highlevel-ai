Why Dental Practices Need Marketing Automation

Most dental practices still rely on word-of-mouth referrals, manual phone call reminders, and outdated postcard recall systems. While these approaches have worked for decades, they leave significant revenue on the table. Patients who do not receive timely reminders miss appointments. Overdue recall patients drift to competitors. New patient inquiries that go unanswered for more than a few minutes are lost.

The average dental practice spends $300–500/mo on a patchwork of separate marketing tools: one for email, another for SMS, a third for appointment scheduling, a fourth for review management, and yet another for landing pages. Each tool has its own login, its own billing, and its own data silo. When patient data does not sync between systems, leads fall through the cracks and staff waste time on manual data entry.

GoHighLevel consolidates all of these functions into a single platform starting at $97/mo. Add roughly $20–30/mo for Twilio SMS and voice, and your total cost is $117–130/mo — a fraction of what most practices currently spend. More importantly, everything runs from one dashboard, which means your front desk team can manage the entire patient communication lifecycle without switching between six different tools.

What GoHighLevel Doesn't Replace

GoHighLevel is a marketing and patient communication platform, not a clinical tool. Be clear about what stays in your existing stack.

Keep Your Existing Clinical Software

GoHighLevel replaces your marketing tools — not your clinical systems. These should remain in your current practice management software:

  • Dental practice management (Dentrix, Eaglesoft, Open Dental)
  • Clinical charting and patient records
  • Insurance billing and claims processing
  • X-ray and imaging systems

Think of GoHighLevel as the marketing and communication layer that sits alongside your clinical software. It handles everything before the patient sits in the chair (acquisition, booking, reminders) and everything after (review requests, recall campaigns) — while your practice management software handles everything during the visit.
