HubSpot's Published Pricing vs Real Costs

HubSpot publishes four tiers for its Marketing Hub, and the pricing page looks straightforward at first glance. But the gap between published pricing and what businesses actually pay is one of the most common frustrations among HubSpot users.

Free tier ($0/mo). HubSpot's free plan includes basic CRM features, limited email marketing (up to 2,000 emails per month), and simple forms. However, it caps you at a small number of marketing contacts, includes HubSpot branding on all forms and emails, and lacks most automation, reporting, and integration features. It is a lead magnet for the platform, not a viable long-term solution for most businesses.

Starter ($20/mo). Removes some branding limitations and adds basic marketing automation. But it is limited to 1,000 marketing contacts, and features like A/B testing, custom reporting, and advanced workflows are not included. For agencies, the Starter tier is too limited to deliver real value to clients.

Professional ($800+/mo). This is the tier where HubSpot becomes a functional marketing platform. It includes marketing automation, custom reporting, A/B testing, and advanced workflows. But the base price of $800/month only covers 2,000 marketing contacts. Every additional 5,000 contacts costs roughly $225/month. An agency managing a client with 20,000 marketing contacts on HubSpot Professional would pay approximately $1,610/month for the Marketing Hub alone.

Enterprise ($3,600+/mo). HubSpot's Enterprise tier adds advanced features like custom objects, behavioral event triggers, predictive lead scoring, and multi-touch revenue attribution. It starts at $3,600/month for 10,000 marketing contacts. Contact overage pricing continues to scale from there. At this tier, HubSpot is competing with Salesforce and Marketo on price.

The core issue is not that HubSpot is a bad platform. It is a mature, feature-rich CRM and marketing suite. The issue is that its pricing model creates a gap between what businesses expect to pay and what they actually pay once they account for contact growth, onboarding requirements, multi-hub needs, and add-on features. For small businesses and agencies, this gap can be significant.

AGENCY IMPACT

What This Means for Agencies

HubSpot's pricing model creates specific challenges for agencies managing multiple clients.

⚠️ Agency Pain Points

  • Multi-client cost explosion: Each HubSpot client typically requires its own portal with its own subscription. An agency managing 10 clients on Marketing Hub Professional is looking at a minimum of $8,000/month in HubSpot subscriptions alone — before contact overages. With contact growth factored in, the number climbs further. There is no multi-client pricing tier that meaningfully reduces the per-portal cost.
  • No white-label option:HubSpot does not offer white-label or rebranding capabilities. Agencies cannot rebrand the HubSpot interface, emails, or client-facing elements with their own company name and logo. Clients always see HubSpot's branding, which makes it difficult for agencies to position themselves as the technology provider rather than a reseller.
  • Per-portal pricing model: Unlike platforms that offer unlimited sub-accounts under a single subscription, HubSpot charges per portal. Even HubSpot's Partner program, while offering discounts, does not eliminate the per-portal cost structure. Agencies scaling from 5 to 50 clients face linear cost growth with no meaningful economy of scale.
  • Onboarding fees multiply: Each new client portal on Professional or Enterprise tier triggers a new onboarding fee. If an agency is setting up 5 new clients on Marketing Hub Professional in a quarter, that is $15,000 in onboarding fees alone on top of the recurring subscription costs.
  • Feature gating pushes upgrades: Basic features like A/B testing emails, custom reporting, and workflow branching logic are gated behind the Professional tier. Agencies frequently start clients on Starter, only to discover within a few months that meaningful marketing automation requires upgrading to Professional — a jump from $20/month to $800/month.
